    RESEARCH OF WIREFRAME

    Upon researching about the usefulness about wire-frames to help with our creations, I learned these things:


    1) Low Fidelity vs High fidelity wire-frames

    -Low Fidelity Wireframes can be created very quickly and are abstract in their complete visual design since it mainly describes the use of space and proximity to maximize the usefulness of the wireframe. 
    -High Fidelity Wireframes have much more detail and information that confirms the substance of the page. There is also usually a clear indication of the actions the website is able to perform in its later stages.
    -For our wireframes, we chose to go with the low fidelity wireframes since we had to create multiple ones to show the client and we figured that the high fidelity ones would be incorporated into the storyboards.

    2) Focus on functions, usability, and user interaction 

    -The low-fidelity wireframes had to be focused on functions mainly. The storyboards would encompass the usability and user interaction. By creating little more detailed low-fidelity wireframes, it could also focus more on the user interaction since it is a very important criteria for us and our client.

    3) Ease in manipulation of design

    -With constant ideas for our website, we needed to make the wireframe template such that it was easily editable and that nothing was set in stone. Therefore, no specific details of the pages were set out on the wireframe.

    4) Clickable wire frames reinforce reliability

    -We did not make clickable wireframes as we thought it would be too time consuming. For future projects, we definitely would make clickable wireframes to enforce reliability in the website mainly for the client.


    5) Saving time and describing client's needs.
